James K. Polk (1845–1849)

James Knox Polk was the 11th president of the United States, serving from 1845 to 1849. He’d been Speaker of the House and Governor of Tennessee.

A protégé of Andrew Jackson, he was a staunch Jacksonian Democrat and guided major territorial expansion.

“Fifty-four Forty or Fight”—expanding the nation’s borders.
